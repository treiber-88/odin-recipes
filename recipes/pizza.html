Supreme Pizza

To some people there is nothing like a mug of beer and a supreme pizza. In this recipe you will find the best way to make a supreme pizza.

And I mean supreme in both senses of the word

Image credit to igorovsyannykov on Pixabay.

Ingredients:

  • Core Ingredients:
  • Dough: 1 lb pizza dough.
  • Sauce: 1/2 to 1 cup marinara or pizza sauce.
  • Cheese: 2-4 cups shredded low-moisture mozzarella, plus Parmesan.
  • Toppings: Sliced pepperoni, cooked Italian sausage, diced bell peppers, sliced onions, sliced mushrooms, and black olives.
    •

    Preparation

  • Prep Toppings: Thinly slice onions, mushrooms, and bell peppers; cook and drain sausage.
  • Prepare Dough: Stretch dough into a 12-16 inch circle.
  • Par-bake: Lightly oil pan, place dough, and bake at 450°F for 5-7 minutes to prevent a soggy center.
  • Assemble: Spread sauce, sprinkle with Parmesan, then add a layer of mozzarella. Add veggies and meats, topped with remaining cheese.
  • Bake: Bake for 12-17 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

    Cheesburgers

    A nice juicy cheesburger says "America" all over it. This is the standard way of making the best cheesburger.

    Image credit to leonardoviera260998 on Pixabay

    Ingredients and Preparation

  • Beef: Use 1 lb of 80/20 ground chuck for four 1/4-lb patties, or 85/15 for slightly leaner options. Do not overwork the meat, which can make it tough.
  • Patty Shape: Form 1/2-inch thick patties. Create a slight depression in the center with your thumb to prevent the burger from bowing in the middle.
  • Seasoning: Season generously with salt and pepper just before cooking.
  • Cheese: Classic American cheese is best for melting.
    •

    Cooking Instructions

  • Preheat a cast-iron skillet or grill to high heat.
  • Cook: Cook for 3 to 5 minutes on the first side without moving it to create a crust.
  • Flip & Cheese: Flip the patty, immediately add one or two slices of American cheese, and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes (for medium).
  • Toast: Toast the buns (preferably brioche or potato rolls) in a separate pan with butter until golden.
  • Rest: Let the patties rest for 2 to 3 minutes before serving.
  • Classic Assembly:
  • Bottom Bun: Spread with mayo, ketchup, or special sauce.
  • Layers: Lettuce (iceberg for crunch), patty with melted cheese, tomato slices, and dill pickles.
  • Top Bun: Add more condiments to the top bun if desired.

    French Fries

    Image credit to msqrd2 on Pixabay
  • Prep: Peel and slice potatoes into even batons (inch thick). Soak them in cold water for at least 30 minutes to remove surface starch, which prevents them from sticking and helps them crisp.
  • The First Fry: Heat oil (peanut or vegetable) to 300°F. Fry the dried potatoes in batches for about 5 minutes until they are tender but still pale.
  • The Second Fry: Increase the oil temperature to 375°F (some suggest up to 400°F). Fry the partially cooked fries again for two to four minutes until they are deep golden brown and very crisp.
  • Finish: Immediately toss with salt in a paper-towel-lined bowl while still hot.
